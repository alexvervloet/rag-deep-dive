# Getting Started with Nimbus Notes

Nimbus Notes is a note-taking app for people who think in messy bursts and want
the mess organized for them. This guide covers your first day.

## Creating your first notebook

When you sign up, Nimbus Notes creates a default notebook called "Inbox." Every
note you capture without choosing a destination lands there, so you never lose a
thought to indecision. You can create additional notebooks from the left sidebar
by clicking the "+" next to NOTEBOOKS. There is no limit to how many notebooks
you can create on any plan.

## Capturing notes

Press Ctrl+N (Cmd+N on macOS) anywhere in the app to start a new note. Notes
support Markdown, checklists, code blocks, and image attachments. The first line
of a note automatically becomes its title.

The Quick Capture window — opened with the global shortcut Ctrl+Shift+Space — lets
you jot a note without switching to the app. Quick Capture always saves to your
Inbox notebook.

## Importing from other apps

You can bring your existing notes with you. From Settings -> Import, Nimbus Notes
can import directly from Evernote (`.enex` files) and Notion (`.zip` workspace
exports). Imported notebooks keep their original folder structure.

If an import fails with error code NN-413, the file exceeds the 50 MB per-note
size limit. Split the offending note or remove large attachments and try again.

## Exporting your notes

Your notes are never locked in. Any notebook can be exported to Markdown, PDF, or
HTML from Settings -> Export. Markdown exports preserve checklists and code
blocks; PDF exports are best for sharing a read-only copy.

## Mobile apps

Nimbus Notes has native apps for iOS (requires iOS 16 or later) and Android
(requires Android 11 or later). Notes sync automatically across every device
signed in to your account, subject to your plan's device limit.
